BIRTHDAY

to the best mother in the world

- Augustina manye Adjoa Mante

Dear Mummy

Happy Birthday to the most incredible mother in the entire world! Today, as I celebrate you, I can’t help but reflect on everything you’ve done for me. From the very first moment I opened my eyes, you’ve been my protector, my teacher, my guide, and my biggest cheerleader. Every single day, you pour out your love and strength to make sure I have the best life possible, and for that, I am endlessly grateful.

You are the person who taught me what love truly means — not through words, but through every sacrifice you’ve made, and every moment you put my happiness before your own. You’ve worked so hard to give me the opportunities I have today, and you never let me feel alone, even in the hardest times. When life felt heavy, you were my strength. When I doubted myself, you believed in me with all your heart. And when I dreamed big, you were the first to tell me that I could achieve it. Everything I am and everything I will ever become is because of you.

I honestly don’t know where I’d be without you. You’ve been my rock, my safe place, and my constant source of comfort and wisdom. You’ve taught me to be kind, to work hard, to never give up, and to always stay true to myself. Those lessons are priceless, and they will stay with me for the rest of my life. No words will ever truly express how much I love you and how much you mean to me, but I hope this message comes close.

On your special day, I just want to say thank you; thank you for being the heart of our family, for filling our home with love and laughter, and for giving me the best gift I could ever ask for: you. You are not just my mother, you are my hero, my inspiration, and my best friend. Today, I celebrate everything that makes you the amazing person you are. I pray that this year brings you as much joy as you’ve given me, because you deserve the world and so much more. Happy Birthday, Mummy, I love you more than words can ever say, and I will forever be grateful to call you my mother.
